Situations that call for flatbed towing

Not every tow requires a flatbed — but these common scenarios do. Here is when Portland drivers call us for flatbed service.

AWD & 4WD Vehicles

Subarus, Audis, and other AWD vehicles can suffer drivetrain damage on a wheel-lift. Flatbed towing is the manufacturer-recommended method for all-wheel drive transport.

Luxury & Exotic Cars

BMWs, Mercedes, Teslas, and high-value vehicles deserve extra care. Our flatbed deck protects paint, body panels, and low-profile tires during loading and transport.

Lowered & Modified Vehicles

Lowered suspensions, body kits, and wide aftermarket wheels need ground clearance that only a flatbed ramp provides. No scraping, no dragging.

Accident Recovery

After a collision, your vehicle may have bent axles, flat tires, or frame damage. Flatbed towing loads the vehicle entirely — no rolling required.

Non-Running Vehicles

Dead engine, seized transmission, or electrical failure? If your vehicle cannot roll, our flatbed winch loads it safely onto the deck.

Long-Distance Transport

Moving a vehicle across the Portland metro to a dealership, body shop, or storage lot? Flatbed towing is the safest choice for any distance.

Flatbed towing keeps all four wheels off the ground during transport, which prevents drivetrain damage. This is especially important for AWD and 4WD vehicles, luxury cars, and lowered vehicles that could scrape on traditional dollies or wheel-lifts.

Yes. Flatbed towing is the recommended method for all-wheel drive and four-wheel drive vehicles. Our flatbed truck loads your vehicle completely, so the drivetrain is never at risk during transport.
